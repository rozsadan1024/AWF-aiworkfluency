## 2. Decision-Maker Analysis

### Who buys training software in Hungarian mid-size companies?

**Primary buyer: HR igazgató / Learning & Development Manager**
- Controls training budget (képzési keret)
- Responsible for employee upskilling compliance
- Accountable for digital transformation HR-side
- Reports to CEO/COO

**Secondary buyer: Ügyvezető (CEO/Managing Director)**
- In companies 50-100 employees: CEO makes ALL software purchasing decisions
- In companies 100-300 employees: CEO approves, HR Director proposes

**Tertiary buyer: COO / Operations Director**
- In manufacturing and logistics: COO owns productivity tools
- Cares about ROI and throughput metrics

**IT Director/Manager**
- Usually involved in SaaS procurement process (security review)
- Rarely the initiator for training tools
- Must sign off if there's SSO/data requirements

**Recommendation: Target HR igazgató first, CC CEO in cold outreach**

## 3. Pain Points (Validated)

[T1: Deloitte Hungary 2025, GVH 2024 — confirmed by multiple sources]

1. **Employees don't know how to use AI tools effectively** — ChatGPT installed, nobody trained
2. **EU AI Act compliance pressure** — AI literacy becoming a legal requirement
3. **Competitive disadvantage fear** — "our competitors are getting faster, we're falling behind"
4. **Language gap** — AI tools are English-first; Hungarian employees struggle
5. **No structured training program** — ad hoc YouTube watching vs. systematic upskilling
6. **ROI uncertainty** — "We don't know if AI investment pays off"
