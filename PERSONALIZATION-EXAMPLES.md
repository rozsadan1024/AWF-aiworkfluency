# AIProof — Personalization Task Examples

**Purpose:** Show users how the same job title gets DIFFERENT tasks based on company/scope.

---

## Task Examples (6 total, 2 duplicates with different scopes)

### 1. Marketing Manager — SaaS Startup (Series A)

**User Input:**
- Job: Marketing Manager
- Company: SaaS startup, 25 employees, Series A funded
- Daily work: Run paid ads, write blog posts, manage 1 contractor
- Tools: HubSpot, Google Ads, Notion

**Generated Task:**
> "Your CEO wants to cut the paid ads budget by 30% but maintain lead volume. Draft a reallocation plan: which channels to pause, which to double down on, and a content strategy to fill the gap. Present it in a Notion doc by EOD."

---

### 2. Marketing Manager — Enterprise B2B (Fortune 500)

**User Input:**
- Job: Marketing Manager
- Company: Fortune 500 manufacturing, enterprise B2B
- Daily work: Coordinate with sales, manage agency partners, report to CMO
- Tools: Salesforce, Marketo, PowerPoint

**Generated Task:**
> "Sales is complaining that marketing leads aren't qualified. Your CMO wants a joint presentation for next week's exec meeting: current lead scoring criteria, proposed changes, and a 90-day implementation plan. Use Salesforce data to back it up."

---

### 3. HR Coordinator — Tech Startup (Remote-First)

**User Input:**
- Job: HR Coordinator
- Company: 80-person remote tech startup
- Daily work: Onboarding, benefits admin, culture initiatives
- Tools: BambooHR, Slack, Zoom

**Generated Task:**
> "A new engineer starts Monday in a different timezone (APAC). Create their full onboarding schedule, coordinate with IT for laptop shipping, draft the welcome Slack message, and set up their first 1:1s with the team. They need to feel connected from day one."

---

### 4. Project Manager — Construction Firm

**User Input:**
- Job: Project Manager
- Company: Regional construction firm, commercial projects
- Daily work: Manage subcontractors, track budgets, coordinate inspections
- Tools: Procore, Excel, email

**Generated Task:**
> "A subcontractor missed the electrical deadline by 3 days, pushing the inspector visit. Draft an email to the client explaining the delay, update the Procore timeline, and propose a recovery plan to get back on track without overtime costs."

---

### 5. Sales Lead — B2B SaaS (Mid-Market)

**User Input:**
- Job: Sales Lead
- Company: B2B SaaS selling to mid-market (50-500 employees)
- Daily work: Manage 4 AEs, demo calls, forecast pipeline
- Tools: Salesforce, Gong, Slack

**Generated Task:**
> "One of your AEs has a $120k deal stuck in legal review for 6 weeks. The prospect's counsel is nitpicking the data processing clause. Draft a Slack message to your AE with 3 tactical moves to unstick it, then write the follow-up email to the prospect's VP suggesting a call."

---

### 6. Sales Lead — Enterprise SaaS (Fortune 1000 Accounts)

**User Input:**
- Job: Sales Lead  
- Company: Enterprise SaaS, selling to Fortune 1000
- Daily work: Strategic accounts, C-level relationships, multi-quarter deals
- Tools: Salesforce, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, PowerPoint

**Generated Task:**
> "A $2M deal with a Fortune 500 retailer is at risk — their new CTO wants to 're-evaluate all vendors.' You have one shot: a 15-minute call with the CTO next Tuesday. Draft the deck outline (max 5 slides), prep 3 objection responses, and write the LinkedIn message to their CFO (your champion) asking for intel."
